Places to Visit Near Panna National Park

Adventure continues beyond the jungle with exciting attractions near Panna.

Khajuraho Temples

Distance from Panna National Park: 44.3 km

This place is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, which is known for its stunning ancient temples built by the Chandela dynasty. The architectural designs of these temples are famous worldwide for their unique Nagara-style design and detailed sculptures that depict various aspects of life. A visit here is a profound journey into India's rich cultural and artistic heritage.

Highlights:

  • A UNESCO World Heritage Site with stunning architecture.
  • World-famous for intricate and detailed stone carvings.
  • Offers a deep insight into India's rich history and culture.

Raneh Falls & Canyon

Distance from Panna National Park: 18.7 km

It is made along the Ken River. The falls are said to be a natural canyon, which is made up of pure crystalline granite that has hues of pink, red, and grey. The force of the waterfall multiplies during the monsoon, making it look like a beautiful cascade falling. As the river makes its way, it cuts through these colourful rock formations, which people come from far places to see.

Highlights:

  • Made up of coloured granite from a canyon
  • In the monsoon, the beauty multiples
  • Perfect for photography and geological wonders

Ken Gharial Sanctuary

Distance from Panna National Park: 22.5 km

On the banks of the Ken River, it is home to the endangered Gharial. A boat ride in the morning or afternoon will make you spot several of them. They come out of the water, and you can see these reptiles basking on sandbanks for sunlight to fall, along with crocodiles and a variety of birds.

Highlights:

  • Protected habitat for the endangered Gharials
  • Boat ride on the Ken River
  • Spot a wide variety of birds and aquatic animals

Diamond Mines (Majhagaon)

Distance from Panna National Park: 55.2 km

Known as the only active Diamond mine in Asia at Majhagaon. As you get a tour here, you will get to know the history of diamond mining, how the formation of diamonds happens according to the different geographical characteristics, and how this region is favourable. Since it is very rare to find these areas, it is named “The Treasure Land”.

Highlights:

  • Get to see the only active diamond mine in Asia
  • Know the history of the mining area
  • A place where geological and industrial tourism boost.
