Things to do

Adventure continues beyond the jungle with exciting attractions near Panna.

Jungle Safari in Panna National Park

The charm of going into national parks is riding a Safari. Ride in an open safari 4x4 vehicle that will take you through the forests with teak and sal trees. A grand canyon will come in the way where Ken river cuts and flows through this.

An experienced guide will accompany you, who will help to track the animals and give you a brief knowledge about the ecosystem. The Majestic Bengal Tiger is the star to spot, along with leopards, sloth bears, deer, sambar, and a wide variety of birds. This way, you get to see the raw beauty of the Indian wilderness.

Ken River Boat Safari

Ever heard of a boat safari? Here you get the chance of both a jungle and a boat safari on the Ken River. It is a peaceful contrast with the jeep safari that lets you spot long-snouted gharials and marsh crocodiles that come out of the water during the noon and bask on the river banks to get the sunlight. Also, you can get to see a variety of birds, especially during the winter time when migratory birds arrive. This spot is also best for photography, which has a mix of calm waters and scenic surroundings in the beauty of the river ecosystem.

Spot the Bengal Tiger

The Panna National Park is widely known for its successful tiger reintroduction program that gives a chance to spot a tiger. They are mostly active in the early morning or late evening when they come near the water bodies to drink water, and this is the best chance to see the Bengal Tiger in their natural habitat. When you get to see a glimpse of this animal the credit goes to the conservation efforts of the wildlife.

Visit the Pandav Falls

The Pandav waterfall is linked with the Mahabharat and it is said that the Pandavas came here during and stayed in caves during their period of exile. The pools near the fall are filled with fish, and it is ideal for photography, relaxation, and a short trek. This place offers cool and peaceful relaxation from the excitement of wildlife hunting.

Raneh Falls Canyon

The Ken River flows through a unique canyon, which is made up of crystalline granite with a mix of different colours. When you stand at the viewpoint, this is where you can get the breathtaking views of the waterfall and the canyon. This is near the Gharial sanctuary. The dramatic sight it offers gives you a lifetime experience.

Experience the Night Safari

In Panna National Park, you can experience the Night Safari, where you can see the nocturnal life. This is very different from daytime activity. If you are lucky enough, you can find creatures like leopards, porcupines, hyenas, foxes, civets, and jungle cats as well. This will add extra excitement to your trip.

Explore the Diamond Mines

In Majhagaon, it is the only active diamond mine in Asia, known for its unique geological and cultural experience. You get to know about the history of diamond mining and the process of how diamonds are extracted. It’s a fascinating glimpse into the industry that gave Panna its fame as the "Diamond District" or the “Treasure of Land”.

Khajuraho Temples

A short drive from the Panna National Park lies the world-famous Khajuraho, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This ancient temple has structures known for its stunning sculptures and beautiful minute artistry that shows the essence of life in the medieval period in India. Our guided tour helps in providing a rich history of India, about its architecture and spiritual traditions.

Birdwatching

Do not forget to bring your binoculars to explore the forests and the riverbank, where you will spot the beautiful birds. There are more than 200 species of birds.
