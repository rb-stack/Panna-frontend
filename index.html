A Glimpse into Panna's Rich History

From ancient temples to royal legacies, explore the historical treasures of Panna.

Origins & Establishment

Pench National Park, spanning the states of Madhya Pradesh and Maharashtra, derives its name from the Pench River that bisects the reserve. The area's conservation history dates back to 1977 when it was first declared a wildlife sanctuary.

The park inspired Rudyard Kipling's "The Jungle Book," making it one of India's most iconic wildlife destinations that continues to captivate visitors worldwide.

Ecological Significance

Pench represents one of central India's most vital tiger habitats, forming part of the Satpura-Maikal tiger landscape. The park's 758 square kilometers protect a diverse ecosystem of teak-dominated forests, grasslands, and riverine habitats.
